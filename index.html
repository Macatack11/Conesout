Guidance Tool Only

ConesOut is a guidance and reference tool to assist trained utility workers in setting out signs, lights and guarding at road works.

This app does NOT replace formal training, a site-specific Risk Assessment, Method Statement, or a Traffic Management Plan approved by the relevant Highway Authority.

All distances and procedures are based on Chapter 8 of the Traffic Signs Manual, TSRGD 2016, NRSWA 1991 and TMA 2004 as guidance only.

Works Vehicle Rules
✅
Park as close to kerb as possible
Min 0.5m from kerb. Wheels turned away from work area so vehicle rolls away from operatives if struck.
✅
Keys in ignition at all times
Vehicle must be movable instantly. Handbrake on, in gear, hazard lights and amber beacon active.
✅
Vehicle between traffic and operatives
The works vehicle acts as a physical buffer. Operatives must NEVER work on the traffic side of the vehicle.
Chapter 8 s.5.4
🚫
NO reversing on live carriageway
Absolute prohibition. If unavoidable, a trained banksman must be in position before any movement.
Chapter 8 s.5.6 - CDM 2015
🚫
NO operative between vehicle and live traffic
Always work on the protected kerb side. Working on the offside of a vehicle on a live road is a serious safety violation.
🚫
NO vehicle parked on approach side of works
Vehicle must be on the departure (downstream) side only - never between advance signs and the works.
Shadow Vehicle (roads 50mph+)
🛡️
Required on all roads 50mph and above
Dedicated vehicle behind works vehicle acting as crash buffer. TMA mandatory on motorways.
Chapter 8 s.5.5 - BD 52
🚦
Arrow board and amber beacon active
Driver stays in cab, seatbelt on at all times. Min 5m gap between shadow and works vehicle.
Reference: Chapter 8 CoP s.5 - HSE HSG185 - CDM 2015 - Road Vehicles (Construction and Use) Regulations - BD 52
